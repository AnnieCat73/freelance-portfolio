Why I made this project

I wanted to take on the challenge of making my first HTML template and making it available initially for free for anybody who might need such a template for a project. I thought a template for a SAAS product would be more interesting but decided to go with accountancy as I know of a potential client who may need one in the future.

Research

Process

I looked at all the various platforms selling templates and found that there weren’t that many for accountancy. They aren’t probably the most popular but the few good ones I saw had really good sales numbers. Business templates in general are among the most popular on these market places.

Idea

I had the idea of having an accountancy firm helping small businesses with their accounting needs. Many small firms struggle with keeping up with their paperwork which may lead to stressful situations. It’s similar to what I do but this is taken from an accounting perspective.

Persona

I created a persona based on a potential client of this accountancy firm, the small business owner.

Goals

  • Help to easily file taxes
  • Alleviate stress by keeping books clean
  • Less stress and predictable finances lead to more time for growing their business

Frustrations

  • Too busy to keep up!
  • Overwhelmed by unending paperwork
  • May be paying too much or worried about penalties if paying too little tax!

About Samantha

Age: 32

Business: Restaurant owner

Location: London

Meet Samantha, a 32-year-old restaurant owner based in London. Sarah has always had a passion for food and decided to turn her passion into a career by opening her own restaurant. However, the day-to-day running of the business has left her with little time to focus on her accounting needs.

Sarah is struggling to keep up with her accounting responsibilities despite her busy schedule running her restaurant. She wants to streamline her accounting process and free up more time to focus on growing her business.

High-Fidelity UI Design

Once the initial wireframes and flows were complete, I started by creating the main landing page. I started by defining the fonts and colors.

Look & feel

To find the right styles, I find listing brand values help me guide me in the right direction. Accountancy falls into the clean, simple and neutral category of websites, the brand values that determined this are:

  • reliable
  • professional
  • efficient
  • trustworthy
  • detail-oriented
  • clean
  • serious
  • balanced

The brand conveys professionalism, trustworthiness and seriousness and this needs to be expressed in the design.

Font

Public Sans

Regular, Medium, Semibold and bold

AaBbCcDdEdFfGgHh

As the brand values have one common ‘feel’ one font will suffice. It has to be a sans serif font as a serif font is more suited for friendlier, luxurious and more formal brands which this is not. Open sans also has a modern look and feel and it is easy to read which makes it well-suited for use in a business context.

Color palette

Accent, primary background, secondary background, secondary background and text, text on light background and error.

These are common brand colors for accountancy websites which I normally would try to stay away from. Having different colors should make you stand out from the crowd. As this is a template whoever will use this template can change the colors to suit their own branding.

Imagery

As this is a template the users will add their own images. I added suitable imagery from pexels.co for the live preview only.

Accessibility Check

This template has been evaluated for contrast to match the AA standards of WCAG.

Original colors chosen:

WCAG AA: Fail

Colors that passed AA standards:

WCAG AA: Pass

My original chosen colors have a more neutral feel. When I added white text on top of them it failed the AA standards. I then had a choice of making a darker variation of each of the colors or to add a dark text on top. Such a brown dark text I don’t think would work well! I made them darker and richer instead.
