The Context And Challenge

Project background and description

  • The Parish Music Box is a band formed in London in 2006. They are best described as a lounge beat combo which is all about the melody, place and story. Inspired by The Smiths, Lambchop and Tindersticks, the released a critically acclaimed debut, “Paradise Is Pocket Sized” some years ago and have ever since been releasing new music and playing the London pub scene and clubs.

The problem - the 'why?' and the plan

  • They came to me as they wanted a showcase or a website for potential bookers to see and hear them.
  • Their main issue was that as a band they enjoy performing and they would like to do more of it.
  • A landing page that ads or social networks can link to could help with this.

Project goals and objectives

  • A website to gather all their info and content in one place so any bookers or general music audience can find out about them more easily.
  • If it’s more easier to find out who they are the chances of getting more bookings should increase.
  • This in turn would mean that they will play more often which is also good revenue for the venues as they already have a following.

The Process And Insight

Research

  • After a brainstorming session with the band where I collected all the that was needed, I made 2 user personas, one for a booker and one for the general music gig goer which resulted in the following:

Primary Actions

  • As a booker/venue I would like to find out more about this band
  • As a booker/venue I would like to book the band
  • As a booker/venue I would like to know if they they are a good fit/are they ‘bookable’?
  • As a music fan/male or female I would like to book know more about this band
  • As a music fan/male or female I would like to see the band live

Secondary Actions

  • As a booker/venue I would like to find out where they are playing next
  • As a booker/venue I would like to find out how I can book them
  • As a music fan/male or female I would like to listen to their music
  • As a music fan/male or female I would like to buy their music

User Stories

  • “Be able to find out easily who they are”
  • “Be able to listen to their music easily”
  • “Be able to watch YouTube clips easily”
  • “Be able to contact them easily”
  • “Be able to book them easily”
  • “Are they any good or are they a good band to book?”
  • “Be able to see where and when they play next”

Annotations

When I always ask myself what is the purpose of the design. A way of defining a hierarchy that will guide the user to push a button is to annotate or add strips that help define the individual user journey visually from top to bottom

It's also a good way to make sure a particular design contains everything it should.

Please see below more information what each strip contains:

  • Emotion

    Humans are visual creatures so often emotional connection comes in the form of an image. An image usually hooks the user and hence strongly influence their decision. A great image of the Parish Music Box playing live should peek a user's interest!

  • Description

    It should give you a validation of the emotional first impression.First confirmation in making the user's decision

  • Details

    Will give user more information to clear any doubts. The description and the details sections should be asking or answering the question is this for me? So here is this a band I want to book or check out?

  • Clarify

    This could be user reviews that will strongly influence the user to make a final decision. If it's good for others it should be good for me!

  • Action

    Once the user reaches this stage, the user should know everything he or she needs to make the final decision. Hopefully any user here will book the band or see them live!
